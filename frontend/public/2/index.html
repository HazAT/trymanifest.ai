Software engineering is being rewritten. Not by new languages or better tooling — by a shift in who does the writing.

Every framework that exists was designed for human hands and human minds. Convention over configuration. Syntactic sugar. Clever abstractions to save keystrokes.

Agents don't need any of that. They need clarity. They need to read a file and know exactly what it does — no hidden behavior, no inherited magic, no documentation to cross-reference.

Manifest is what you get when you strip everything back to what matters. No dependencies. No abstractions. What you read is what you get.

"There are no docs because there is nothing to document. The code is the documentation."

Meet Spark.

Why build with agents and then stop?

Spark runs alongside your application. Dev, staging, production — it doesn't matter. When something breaks, Spark knows. When something needs attention, Spark acts.

Think of it as a notification system for agents. Errors, crashes, failures — routed to an intelligence that can read your codebase, understand the context, and make decisions.

Agents monitoring your application, making decisions to serve your vision. Run with them.
